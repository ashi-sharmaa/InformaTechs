What is Robotics?

Robotics is the design and construction of robots that combines computer science, computer engineering, and electronics. There are five major fields of robotics: operator interface, mobility or locomotion, manipulators and effectors, programming, and sensing and perception, all of which contribute to the maintenance and design of all robots. Robots have applications in various fields, including medicine, machinery, and even underwater exploration. Prosthetics, a sub-branch of robotics, for example, are used in the medical field as substitutes for body parts, allowing individuals to perform daily tasks more easily. Robotics engineers, mechanical engineers, and software engineers are examples of jobs that incorporate different aspects of robotics.


Potential Career Fields:

Robotics Engineer: Robotics engineers are experts in designing and developing robotic prototypes for a variety of fields. They also work to ensure that the robots operate safely and without malfunctions through various forms of testing and maintenance checks.

Biomedical Engineer: Biomedical engineers use robotics in the subfield of prosthetics, for example, as a supplement to help amputees with daily tasks. Furthermore, advanced technology-based robots can be used to interact with patients and collect vital signs.

Mechanical Engineer: Mechanical engineers can work in robotics in the same way that robotics engineers do: designing and manufacturing robotic components for various industries, such as high-temperatures and welding.

Design Engineer: Design Engineers work on aspects of the robot's outward appearance by sketching layouts and blueprints to mimic the final product. They frequently collaborate with mechanical engineers to ensure that their designs are efficient and practical.

Software Engineer: Software engineers work on the software that powers and functions the robots. They frequently apply computer science principles and are comfortable coding, debugging, and testing their work.

Hardware Engineer: Hardware Engineers create and test the physical components of robots, such as motherboards and electrical circuits that power the robotic system. They frequently collaborate with Software Engineers to ensure that the physical components correspond with the technical system.

Electromechanical and Robotics Technician: These technicians manage robotic systems such as drones, automated machines, and electromechanical systems by utilizing mechanical and electrical properties.

Computer Scientist: Computer Scientists, like Software Engineers, use advanced computer programming to create the desired robot functions. They also work with aspects of machine learning, such as artificial intelligence, to develop autonomous robots.

Impact of Robotics on the World

Robotic machinery has the potential to reduce the number of hazardous jobs that humans are required to perform, such as lifting heavy loads and performing repetitive tasks. Robots can help improve the world of medicine by using advanced medical technology such as prosthetics, and autonomous robots, such as AI, can assist in keeping track of medical records and even diagnosing patients with medical conditions after conditioning and testing the software. Overall, they can aid in the reduction of repetitive and time-consuming tasks for humans, freeing up more time for innovation.
