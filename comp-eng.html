What is Computer Engineering?

Computer Engineering is a discipline that combines elements of computer science and electrical engineering to design computer systems and hardware components. More specifically, it focuses on the integration of hardware and software to create efficient and reliable computer systems and devices.

Computer Engineering encompasses a broad range of specialized areas including computer architecture, digital design systems, embedded systems, computer network, and operating systems. Computer Engineers work on both the hardware and software aspects off a computer system.

A day-to-day example of what a computer engineer does will vary depending on their specialization, the industry they work in, and specific projects they are involved in. According to data from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median salary of computer hardware engineers (which includes computer engineering) was around $120,000 USD as of May 2020.

Most tech companies such as Google, Amazon, Intel, Oracle, or Adobe, to name a few, frequently hire computer engineering alumnis.

Potential Job Overview

Business Systems Analyst: Business Systems Analysts are professionals who bridge the gap between business needs and technology solutions within an organization. They are involved in various activities throughout the software development life cycle such as: analytics, design and modeling, testing and quality assurance, user training and support, and finally, coordinating and managing projects.

Hardware Systems Designer: A computer hardware systems designer is responsible for designing and developing computer hardware systems and components. They focus on the physical aspects of computer systems, including the design and integration of various hardware components to create functional and efficient systems.

Computer Security Analyst:A computer security analyst, also known as a cybersecurity analyst, is responsible for protecting computer systems, networks, and data from security threats. They work to identify vulnerabilities, assess risks, and implement security measures to safeguard against potential attacks or breaches.

Satellite Communications Specialist: Satellite communications specialists work with satellites, ground stations, and associated equipment to establish reliable and efficient communication links for various applications such as telecommunications, broadcasting, remote sensing and navigation. They are responsible for designing, implementing, and maintaining satellite communication systems.

Telecommunication Engineer: Telecommunication Engineers focus on the efficiency and maintenance of systems that transfer data, voice, and multimedia messages over long distances. They contribute to industries such as telecommunication service providers, network equipment manufacturers, government agencies (EW), research organizations, and other large organizations that require broad and secure networks.

Database Developer: Database developers work closely with data scientists and database management stems (DBMS) to create and manga structured data that supports the storage retrieval and manipulation of information for various applications and organizations
